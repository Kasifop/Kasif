The Void Soul

Chapter 1: The Beginning of End

The sky was not black, but a deep, bruised purple that suggested the universe itself was bleeding. Kael stood on the precipice of the shattered world, looking down into the abyss that had once been his home. The wind howled, not with the sound of air rushing past stone, but with the whispers of a billion lost souls.

"It is time," the voice echoed. It didn't come from behind him, but from inside his own mind—a cold, metallic resonance that vibrated through his very bones.

He tightened his grip on the hilt of the Obsidian Blade. The metal was cold enough to burn, pulsating with a faint, rhythmic heartbeat. This was the curse of immortality: not to live forever, but to remember everything that had died.

Steps echoed on the crystalline floor behind him. He didn't turn. He knew who it was. Only one other entity could survive in this vacuum of reality.

"You cannot stop the cycle, Kael," the figure said, its voice smooth like velvet over gravel. "The stars must die so that the void may feed. It is the only truth."

Kael finally turned. His eyes, once human, were now pools of swirling liquid silver. "Then let the void choke on my wrath."

The horizon exploded into silence.

The darkness surged forward, a tidal wave of nothingness that sought to erase the very concept of existence. Kael did not flinch. He had seen civilizations rise from dust and return to it in the blink of a cosmic eye. What was one more apocalypse to a man who had forgotten how to count the years?

He drew the blade. The sound was like a scream cut short. The purple sky cracked, fissures of blinding white light tearing through the fabric of the dimension. It was beautiful. It was terrifying. It was the end.
